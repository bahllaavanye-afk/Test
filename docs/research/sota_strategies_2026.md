# SOTA Quantitative Trading Strategies — Research Report (June 2026)

**Prepared for:** QuantEdge (Alpaca/TradeStation equities, Binance crypto, Polymarket prediction markets)
**Scope:** Crypto market-making & microstructure, prediction-market strategies, ML alpha, on-chain/alt data, execution & portfolio. All findings are graded for retail viability ($10k–$1M) and checked against QuantEdge's existing strategy/ML inventory.
**Honesty note:** Vendor blogs and Medium posts dominate some niches (especially crypto arb and on-chain metrics); claimed Sharpes there should be assumed inflated. Peer-reviewed / arXiv walk-forward results are flagged explicitly.

## TL;DR — Top 5 Actionable Recommendations (not currently implemented)

### 1. Polymarket CLOB market making with liquidity-reward farming — **highest priority**
Polymarket launched CLOB v2 (April 2026) with a $1M liquidity-rewards program: makers pay zero fees, receive 20–25% of taker fees as daily rebates, and earn formula-based rewards that strongly favor two-sided quoting (single-sided quotes are penalized 3x). Practitioners report $150–300/day per active market on $100K+ daily-volume markets ([Polymarket docs](https://docs.polymarket.com/market-makers/liquidity-rewards), [crypto.news](https://crypto.news/polymarket-rolls-out-clob-v2-with-1m-liquidity-rewards-to-harden-prediction-markets/), [startpolymarket.com](https://startpolymarket.com/strategies/market-making/)). This is subsidized yield — the closest thing to structural edge available at retail scale right now, and QuantEdge already has Polymarket connectivity and a CLOB client for its binary-arb strategy.
**Implementation sketch:** New plugin in `backend/app/strategies/` (per the zero-cross-coupling plugin pattern) reusing the existing Polymarket broker adapter. Quote both YES and NO sides inside the qualifying spread band around midpoint, with inventory skew logic borrowed from a simplified Avellaneda-Stoikov reservation price. Cap inventory per market via the existing CVaR risk layer; classify under the 70% arbitrage bucket. Resolution risk is the tail: hard-stop quoting when time-to-resolution < N hours or when the existing HMM/news triggers fire. Run 2 weeks paper-first per platform policy. Complexity: **Medium**. Capital: works from ~$5–10k across a handful of markets.

### 2. Polymarket ↔ Kalshi cross-platform arbitrage
Identical events diverge >5 percentage points ~15–20% of the time; bots target 2.5–3% spreads to clear ~3–4% combined fees, netting ~$0.01–0.02 per share pair. An estimated **$40M in arb profits was extracted Apr 2024–Apr 2025**, mostly by bots ([AhaSignals](https://ahasignals.com/research/prediction-market-arbitrage-strategies/), [laikalabs.ai](https://laikalabs.ai/prediction-markets/polymarket-kalshi-arbitrage-guide), open-source reference bot: [ImMike/polymarket-arbitrage](https://github.com/ImMike/polymarket-arbitrage)). This is the natural extension of QuantEdge's existing single-platform YES+NO < $0.97 arb. Honest caveat: it's an arms race; edge per fill is thin and leg risk is real — treat first-month paper results as the go/no-go gate.
**Implementation sketch:** Add a Kalshi broker adapter in the brokers module (REST + websocket, regulated US exchange — simpler auth than Polymarket's wallet signing). Build an event-matching service (LLM-assisted fuzzy matching of market titles/resolution criteria, human-confirmed mapping table — settlement-rule mismatches are the main loss source). Extend the existing binary-arb strategy to scan the cross-platform mapping; execute the cheaper leg first, hedge leg with IOC orders, unwind on partial fills. Complexity: **Medium-High** (mostly the matching/settlement-rule layer). Capital: $10k–$100k; scales poorly beyond that.

### 3. High-probability "bond" strategy on Polymarket (favorite-longshot bias harvesting)
Academic work on 1.4M users / $20B+ volume confirms persistent favorite-longshot bias: high-priced contracts resolve *more* often than priced, low-priced ones less. Systematically buying near-certain contracts (> $0.95) close to resolution — a "bond" strategy — accounted for **$40M+ in aggregate low-risk profits**, while longshot buying (<$0.10) is the primary driver of retail losses ([QuantPedia](https://quantpedia.com/systematic-edges-in-prediction-markets/), [Akey et al. 2025 coverage](https://predictionnews.com/news/study-reveals-nuance-behind-polymarket-90-percent-accuracy-rate/), [ResearchGate, Dec 2025](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/398660802_Exploring_Decentralized_Prediction_Markets_Accuracy_Skill_and_Bias_on_Polymarket)). Edge per trade is small (1–4%) but annualizes well via short holding periods. The killer risk is resolution/oracle disputes — one bad resolution wipes out ~25 winners.
**Implementation sketch:** New strategy plugin scanning all Polymarket markets for price ≥ $0.95–0.98 with resolution < 7–14 days; rank by annualized yield net of spread; exclude UMA-dispute-prone categories (subjective resolution criteria) via a static blocklist plus an LLM screen of resolution text. Position sizing through the existing Kelly module with a hard per-market cap (e.g., 2% of bucket) since outcomes are binary. Sits in the arb bucket; fully automatable against the existing CLOB client. Complexity: **Low** — fastest to ship of the five. Capital: any size from $5k.

### 4. Avellaneda-Stoikov market making on Binance perps, gated by VPIN order-flow toxicity
A-S remains the canonical crypto MM framework; recent work (Stoikov et al., Dec 2024) develops risk parameters for live perp market making from candle data ([SSRN](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=5066176), [Hummingbot guide](https://medium.com/hummingbot/a-comprehensive-guide-to-avellaneda-stoikovs-market-making-strategy-102d64bf5df6), [DolphinDB backtest tutorial](https://docs.dolphindb.com/en/Tutorials/market_making_strategies.html)). MM does well in mean-reverting regimes and bleeds in trends — which is exactly what VPIN addresses: 2024–2026 studies show VPIN spikes precede Bitcoin price jumps and stress episodes ([Bitcoin wild moves, RIBAF 2026](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0275531925004192), [Buildix explainer](https://www.buildix.trade/blog/what-is-vpin-flow-toxicity-crypto-trading)); practitioner rule-of-thumb: widen/pull quotes when VPIN > ~0.7, resume below ~0.3. No published net-Sharpe at retail scale — expect thin edge on majors; favor mid-cap perps where spreads are wider.
**Implementation sketch:** Strategy plugin + a new `vpin.py` feature in `backend/app/ml/` features (volume-bucketed trade classification from the Binance trade stream — also reusable as a regime feature by the existing HMM). Quote via reservation-price + optimal-spread formulas with γ (risk aversion) and inventory limits wired into the CVaR layer; kill-switch on VPIN spike, funding-window crossings, and the existing liquidation-cascade detector (natural synergy — that strategy already detects the toxic flow MM must avoid). Complexity: **High** (needs low-latency order management, maker-fee tier awareness). Capital: $25k+ per market to quote meaningful size. Bucket: arbitrage/market-neutral.

### 5. Predictive (ML-driven) funding-rate harvesting — upgrade of the existing static funding arb
QuantEdge has static funding-rate arb. The published gap between static and predictive versions is large: a static threshold strategy (enter >0.01%/8h, exit <0.005%) backtested ~18%/yr, Sharpe ~1.4 (2019–2023), while ML-predicted funding 4h ahead with confidence-weighted sizing reached ~31%/yr, Sharpe ~2.3 ([CoinCryptoRank guide](https://coincryptorank.com/blog/funding-rate-arbitrage); practitioner backtests claim Sharpe 2–6, e.g. [Keel template, Sharpe 2.17 over 20 months](https://usekeel.io/learn/funding-rate) — treat the high end as marketing). Important context: simple carry has compressed hard — CME annualized basis is ~5% vs ~4.5% risk-free in 2026 and academic work finds crypto carry Sharpe turned negative in 2025 ([crypto.news](https://crypto.news/cme-bitcoin-futures-slump-as-basis-trade-unwinds-and-wall-street-steps-back/), [arXiv 2510.14435](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2510.14435)). The remaining edge is in *cross-sectional selection* (rotating into the highest predicted funding across hundreds of alt perps) and *timing*, not in passively holding BTC carry.
**Implementation sketch:** Train the existing LightGBM pipeline in `backend/app/ml/` to predict next-1/8h funding per perp (features: current funding, OI change, basis, long/short ratio, realized vol, VPIN). The strategy plugin ranks the universe, holds the top-N spot-long/perp-short pairs, sizes by prediction confidence via Kelly, and exits pre-emptively on predicted sign flips. Walk-forward validation only, per platform policy. Complexity: **Medium** (mostly feature plumbing; execution already exists). Capital: $50k+ for meaningful diversification across alts. Bucket: arbitrage (70%).

**Runners-up:** LLM news-sentiment factor for equities (strong published Sharpes but likely overfit — see §3), token-unlock calendar shorts (good event evidence, borrow costs eat the edge — see §4), regime-conditional capital allocation across strategies (partially covered by existing HMM — see §5).

## 1. Crypto Market-Making & Microstructure (2024–2026)

### 1.1 Avellaneda-Stoikov on perps
- The A-S model (inventory-risk-managed quoting via reservation price + optimal spread) is now standard in crypto; Hummingbot ships it as a default strategy ([guide](https://medium.com/hummingbot/a-comprehensive-guide-to-avellaneda-stoikovs-market-making-strategy-102d64bf5df6)). Stoikov and co-authors published a Dec 2024 paper deriving live-trading risk parameters for perp MM from candlestick data ([SSRN 5066176](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=5066176)).
- Backtesting on BTC/USDT perps is documented end-to-end (tick replay, queue position) by [DolphinDB](https://docs.dolphindb.com/en/Tutorials/market_making_strategies.html); an RL-tuned A-S variant beat the vanilla model in a [2022 peer-reviewed study](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC9767337/).
- Consistent finding: A-S profits in mean-reverting regimes, loses in trends; volatility widens the reservation-price offset ([Algotron practical guide](https://algotron.medium.com/avellaneda-stoikov-market-making-strategy-a-practical-guide-for-crypto-traders-d42d0682c6d1)).
- **Assessment:** Edge: low single-digit bps per round trip, plus maker rebates; Sharpe undisclosed at retail scale. Complexity **High**. Data: free (exchange websockets). Retail-viable on mid-cap perps; on BTC/ETH you compete with HFT firms. **QuantEdge has it: No.**

### 1.2 Cross-exchange arbitrage
- Consensus across 2025 sources: still profitable only for fast, well-capitalized operators. Fees consume 30–50% of gross edge for retail; opportunities last <10 seconds; realistic minimum working capital ~$100k ([CoinAPI](https://www.coinapi.io/blog/crypto-arbitrage-explained-coinapi-profit-opportunities-2025), [dCentralab](https://www.dcentralab.com/blog/what-is-crypto-arbitrage-trading-a-traders-guide-for-2025), [Bitsgap](https://bitsgap.com/blog/crypto-arbitrage-explained-tutorial)).
- **Assessment:** Largely **not viable** at QuantEdge's latency profile (single-exchange Binance connectivity, no colocated infra). Edge ~0; complexity High. **Recommend skipping.** QuantEdge's existing *triangular* arb (single-exchange, no transfer risk) is the right variant to keep. **QuantEdge has cross-exchange: No — and shouldn't.**

### 1.3 Basis / carry
- 2026 status: CME annualized basis ~5% vs ~4.5% risk-free; basis-trade open interest at 2-year lows; the ETF-era cash-and-carry trade has largely unwound ([crypto.news](https://crypto.news/cme-bitcoin-futures-slump-as-basis-trade-unwinds-and-wall-street-steps-back/), [CryptoQuant chart](https://cryptoquant.com/asset/btc/chart/derivatives/cme-futures-annualized-basis)). Academic survey: crypto carry "profitability compressed sharply since 2024" and **Sharpe turned negative in 2025** ([arXiv 2510.14435](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2510.14435)).
- **Assessment:** Passive BTC/ETH carry is dead money at current rates. Alt-perp funding dispersion is where residual edge lives (see TL;DR #5). **QuantEdge has static funding arb: Yes; predictive/cross-sectional: No.**

### 1.4 VPIN order-flow toxicity
- Recent crypto-specific evidence: VPIN spikes precede Bitcoin price jumps and stress episodes; Easley et al. (2024) use VPIN to characterize/predict dynamics across major coins ([RIBAF 2026 paper](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0275531925004192), [ResearchGate version](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/396478814_Bitcoin_wild_moves_evidence_from_order_flow_toxicity_and_price_jumps)). Practitioner thresholds: >0.7 = toxic, directional move likely; <0.3 post-spike = absorbed, reversion likely ([Buildix](https://www.buildix.trade/blog/what-is-vpin-flow-toxicity-crypto-trading)). Open-source reference implementation: [yt-feng/VPIN](https://github.com/yt-feng/VPIN).
- **Assessment:** Best used as a **filter/feature**, not a standalone strategy — quote-pulling for MM, entry gating for mean-reversion strategies, and a feature for the HMM regime model. Complexity **Low-Medium**; data free. **QuantEdge has it: No.**

## 2. Prediction-Market Strategies (2024–2026)

### 2.1 Cross-platform arb (Polymarket/Kalshi) — see TL;DR #2
Key numbers: >5pt divergence 15–20% of the time; net $0.01–0.02/pair after ~3–4% fees; $40M aggregate bot profits Apr 2024–Apr 2025 ([AhaSignals](https://ahasignals.com/research/prediction-market-arbitrage-strategies/), [polycopy](https://polycopy.app/polymarket-kalshi-arbitrage), [eventarb calculator](https://www.eventarb.com/)). Manifold is play-money — ignore it for P&L (useful only as a free sentiment signal).

### 2.2 Near-certain buying / longshot-bias harvesting — see TL;DR #3
The strongest academic result in the niche: top 1% of wallets capture 84% of gains; <30% of traders profit; longshot buying is the dominant retail loss source; high-probability "bond" buying produced $40M+ in low-risk profits ([Akey, Grégoire, Harvie & Martineau 2025, coverage](https://predictionnews.com/news/study-reveals-nuance-behind-polymarket-90-percent-accuracy-rate/), [Glancszpigel, "Debunking the Polymarket Dream"](https://fglancszpigel.medium.com/debunking-the-polymarket-dream-d67ba3922e4b), [QuantPedia](https://quantpedia.com/systematic-edges-in-prediction-markets/)).

### 2.3 News-latency momentum
- Bots exploiting the lag between exchange prices/news and Polymarket quotes are highly profitable: one bot extracted **$271.5k in 30 days** from a multi-second UI/price lag; 14 of the top-20 leaderboard wallets are bots; AI agents are >30% of wallet activity with ~37% profitable vs 7–13% of humans ([Predik](https://predik.io/en/blog/bot-trading-polymarket-exploit-latencia-arbitraje-en), [Finance Magnates](https://www.financemagnates.com/trending/prediction-markets-are-turning-into-a-bot-playground/)).
- **But:** Polymarket added dynamic taker fees on short-dated crypto markets specifically to kill latency arb ([Finance Magnates](https://www.financemagnates.com/cryptocurrency/polymarket-introduces-dynamic-fees-to-curb-latency-arbitrage-in-short-term-crypto-markets/)). The 5–15-minute crypto-price markets are now a fee-gated arms race — **avoid**. Longer-dated news momentum (court rulings, data releases → political/econ markets) remains viable at human-plus-API latency. Complexity Medium; edge episodic. **QuantEdge has it: No.**

### 2.4 Market making on thin CLOB books — see TL;DR #1
Subsidized by the $1M rewards program + 20–25% taker-fee rebates; two-sided quoting strongly favored by the scoring formula ([Polymarket docs](https://docs.polymarket.com/market-makers/liquidity-rewards), [Medium deep-dive](https://medium.com/mountain-movers/the-hidden-yield-layer-on-polymarket-how-maker-rebates-holding-rewards-and-liquidity-incentives-e2e41972dcb7)). The hype flag: "$150–300/day per market" claims come from practitioner blogs, not audited track records; reward pools dilute as more makers join.

## 3. ML Alpha (2024–2026): what actually beats LSTM/TFT

### 3.1 Mamba/SSM — incremental, real
- S-Mamba, MambaTS, CMDMamba and hybrids consistently beat Transformer baselines (PatchTST, FEDformer) on standard long-horizon benchmarks at near-linear cost ([CMDMamba, 2025](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12303894/), [S-Mamba overview](https://www.emergentmind.com/topics/s-mamba-model)); a 2026 Mamba-Transformer hybrid validates on CSI 300/800 stock data 2008–2024 ([ACM](https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3772900.3772968)).
- **Caveat:** improvements are in forecast RMSE on benchmark datasets, not net-of-cost trading Sharpe; financial gains over a tuned LSTM/TFT are typically marginal. **QuantEdge has it: Yes (SSM/Mamba-style model exists)** — low marginal value in chasing newer variants.

### 3.2 Time-series foundation models (TimesFM, Chronos) — **negative result, important**
- The most rigorous evaluation to date ([Rahimikia, "Re(Visiting) Time Series Foundation Models in Finance", arXiv 2511.18578](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2511.18578)): zero-shot Chronos-large gets OOS R² of **−1.37%**, TimesFM-500M **−2.80%**, directional accuracy ~50–51%; both **underperform CatBoost/LightGBM**; fine-tuning on financial data fails to close the gap; results replicate across 12 more TSFM architectures.
- **Recommendation: do NOT invest in TSFMs for return forecasting.** QuantEdge's existing LightGBM/XGBoost stack is, per this evidence, the stronger baseline. This is the clearest hype-flag in the report.

### 3.3 LLM-based signals
- *News sentiment:* Kirtac & Germano report Sharpe 3.05 / +355% (Aug 2021–Jul 2023) for an OPT-based long-short on news sentiment, beating FinBERT (2.07) and dictionary methods (1.23) ([SSRN](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=4706629), [Finance Research Letters](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S1544612324002575)). OOS extensions through Jan 2025 stay positive but degrade ([arXiv 2412.19245](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2412.19245)). A hierarchical RL+LLM portfolio framework reports 26% ann. / Sharpe 1.2 on 2018–2024 held-out data ([HARLF, arXiv](https://arxiv.org/html/2507.18560v1)).
- **Hype flag:** short samples, 10bps assumed costs, and small/mid-cap tilt mean live Sharpe is realistically 1/3–1/2 of published. Still, this is the most credible *new* alpha family for QuantEdge's equity book. Complexity Medium (news feed cost is the real constraint: free via RSS/EDGAR, better via paid feeds). **QuantEdge has it: No.**
- *LLM alpha-factor mining* (Alpha-GPT, AlphaAgent, Chain-of-Alpha, QuantaAlpha): agentic factor-generation loops with regularization against crowding show better IC/ICIR than baselines on 2021–2025 Chinese + US equity data ([AlphaAgent, arXiv 2502.16789](https://arxiv.org/html/2502.16789v2), [MCTS framework, arXiv 2505.11122](https://arxiv.org/html/2505.11122v2), [open-source QuantaAlpha](https://github.com/QuantaAlpha/QuantaAlpha)). Treat as a **research-productivity tool** (generating candidate factors for the existing walk-forward pipeline), not a strategy. Complexity Medium; cost = LLM tokens. **QuantEdge has it: No.**

## 4. On-Chain / Alternative Data

- **MVRV Z-score / netflow:** cycle-timing indicators with strong descriptive track records (MVRV called every major top/bottom since 2011; 30d-MA net outflows >20k BTC/month preceded every major rally since 2020) ([Bitcoin Magazine Pro](https://www.bitcoinmagazinepro.com/charts/mvrv-zscore/), [spotedcrypto](https://www.spotedcrypto.com/bitcoin-onchain-bottom-signals-march-2026/)). **But** there are no rigorous walk-forward, cost-adjusted academic backtests — only vendor charts, and every metric "works in some regimes and fails in others." Best use: low-frequency regime features for the HMM, not standalone signals. Data free (BGeometrics, CryptoQuant free tier). Complexity Low. **QuantEdge has it: No.**
- **Token unlock calendar:** event-study evidence is decent — June 2024: ARB −30%, APT −26%, STRK −38% in the 30 days around major cliff unlocks; Keyrock studied 16,000+ unlocks finding price pressure concentrated in large (>1% supply) investor/team cliff unlocks, with much of the move *front-running the date* ([KuCoin summary](https://www.kucoin.com/news/articles/token-unlock-schedule-why-unlocking-tokens-doesn-t-always-trigger-price-drops), [Keyrock](https://keyrock.com/from-locked-to-liquidity-what-16000-token-unlocks-teach-us/), free calendars: [CryptoRank](https://cryptorank.io/token-unlock), [CoinGlass](https://www.coinglass.com/token-unlock), [Tokenomist](https://tokenomist.ai/)). Implementation: short perps 7–30 days pre-unlock on >1%-supply cliff events, exit at unlock. The catch: funding costs on crowded shorts routinely exceed the expected drop — the trade is crowded precisely because the calendar is public. Edge: episodic, modest; complexity Low; viable at $10k+. **QuantEdge has it: No** — worthwhile as a small-allocation event strategy.
- **Whale tracking / copy trading:** Nansen-style smart-money labels are descriptive; even Nansen warns direct copying fails on latency, gas, and impact ([Nansen](https://www.nansen.ai/post/top-smart-money-indicators-in-crypto-how-to-identify-and-track-whale-activity), [Ledger](https://www.ledger.com/academy/topics/crypto/how-to-track-crypto-whale-movements)). No credible published evidence of persistent retail-scale alpha. **Skip as a strategy; optional as a feature.**

## 5. Execution & Portfolio

- **RL execution vs TWAP:** RL-Exec beats TWAP and book-liquidity VWAP on BTC-USD LOB replays by **+2–3bps (30min) to +23bps (120min)** ([arXiv 2511.07434](https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.07434)); a PPO-CNN agent achieves 2.13bps arrival slippage, statistically significant vs benchmarks ([arXiv 2601.04896](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2601.04896)); other 2024–2026 studies report TWAP improvements of ~3–18bps ([Springer](https://link.springer.com/chapter/10.1007/978-3-030-67670-4_14), [ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0957417424011291)). **QuantEdge has PPO RL execution: Yes** — the marginal upgrade is impact-aware reward shaping and longer-horizon scheduling per RL-Exec; worthwhile only once order sizes exceed ~0.1% of interval volume.
- **Regime-conditional allocation:** 2024–2026 results are consistently positive: HMM/jump-model regime overlays deliver **Sharpe uplifts of ~0.4–0.7 and 10–20pt drawdown reductions** out-of-sample across multi-asset universes ([overview](https://www.emergentmind.com/topics/regime-based-portfolio-allocation), [HMM+RL, arXiv 2605.27848](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.27848), [statistical jump models, arXiv 2402.05272](https://arxiv.org/html/2402.05272v2), [jump model + MPC, MDPI](https://www.mdpi.com/2227-7390/13/17/2837)). **QuantEdge has HMM regime detection: Yes — but the high-value move is wiring it to *capital allocation across strategies*** (e.g., throttle momentum/breakout in high-vol regimes, upweight MM/mean-reversion), i.e., a regime-conditional layer on top of the existing HRP/CVaR sizing. Complexity Low-Medium since both components already exist.
